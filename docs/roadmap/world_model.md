# World Models

## What is this topic?

World models aim to learn structured representations of environment dynamics, object states, agent interactions, and future outcomes, so that an embodied system can predict, plan, and act more effectively.

## Why it matters

A strong world model can support:

- future prediction
- planning before acting
- data-efficient learning
- long-horizon reasoning
- transfer across tasks

## Core Problems

- representation learning for physical environments
- latent dynamics modeling
- action-conditioned future prediction
- object-centric or scene-centric modeling
- integration with control and reinforcement learning
- transfer across embodiments

## Typical Technical Routes

- latent dynamics models
- video prediction and generative modeling
- action-conditioned sequence modeling
- object-centric world models
- 3D or geometry-aware world models
- language-conditioned world models

## Key Challenges

- long-horizon consistency
- physical plausibility
- embodiment transfer
- scalability
- interaction modeling
- bridging prediction and control
