# RSS

## Why watch RSS

RSS remains one of the most selective robotics venues. It is often where method papers with strong systems credibility land before they diffuse into broader embodied-AI discussion.

## Official entry

https://roboticsconference.org/

## 2025 embodied AI paper picks

- [Dex1B: Learning with 1B Demonstrations for Dexterous Manipulation](https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/rss21/p106.html)
  RSS 2025. Important for dexterous manipulation because it pushes data scale aggressively instead of assuming small curated hand datasets.

- [Physics-Driven Data Generation for Contact-Rich Manipulation via Trajectory Optimization](https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/rss21/p053.html)
  RSS 2025. Useful if you care about cross-embodiment data generation and contact-rich manipulation without paying the full price of manual teleoperation.

- [Implicit Neural-Representation Learning for Elastic Deformable-Object Manipulations](https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/rss21/p035.html)
  RSS 2025. A good deformable-manipulation entry because it ties representation learning directly to control on elastic objects.

- [PIN-WM: Learning Physics-INformed World Models for Non-Prehensile Manipulation](https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/rss21/p153.pdf)
  RSS 2025. Worth reading if you want a more physics-aware world-model story for pushing and poking, not only grasp-based tasks.

- [Bridging the Sim-to-Real Gap for Athletic Loco-Manipulation](https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/rss21/p125.html)
  RSS 2025. High-signal paper for readers who care about whole-body manipulation and athletic robot behavior, not only tabletop execution.
